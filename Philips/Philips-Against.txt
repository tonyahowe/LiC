                <stanza>
                    <l>There's no such thing as pleasure here,</l>
                    <l><ref target="a001" note="f001">'Tis all a perfect cheat</ref><note xml:id="n001" target="a001">Philips is insinuating that pleasure isn't real. It is a false concept.</note>,</l>
                    <l>Which does but shine and disappear,</l>
                    <l>Whose charm is but deceit:</l>
                    <l><ref target="a002" corresp="n002">The empty bribe of yielding souls</ref><note xml:id="n002" target="a002">An insinuation that those without godly souls find it easy to succumb to pleasure.</note>,</l>
                    <l><ref target="a003" corresp="n003">Which first betrays, and then controls</ref><note xml:id="n003" target="a003">Once someone has the taste of pleasure, they are fully gripped by it.</note>.</l>
                </stanza>
                
                <stanza>
                    <l>'Tis true, it looks at distance fair,</l>
                    <l>But if we do approach,</l>
                    <l>The fruit of <ref target="a004" corresp="n004">Sodom</ref><note xml:id="n004" target="a004">Sodom was a city destroyed by God for their sins.</note>will impair,</l>
                    <l>And perish at a touch;</l>
                    <l>It being than in fancy less,</l>
                    <l>And we expect more than possess.</l>
                </stanza>
                
                <stanza>
                    <l>For by our pleasure we are <ref target="a005" corresp="n005">cloy'd</ref><note xml:id="n005" target="a005">Cloy'd refers to being made weary by something that was initially pleasureable or sweet.</note></l>
                    <l>And so desire is done;</l>
                    <l>Or else, like rivers, they make wide</l>
                    <l>The channels where they run;</l>
                    <l>And either way true bliss destroys,</l>
                    <l>Making us narrow, or our joys.</l>
                </stanza>
                
                <stanza>
                    <l>We covet pleasure easily,</l>
                    <l>But ne'er true <ref target="a006" corresp="n006">bliss possess</ref><note xml:id="n006" target="a006">Bliss cannot be attained by something as blinding and detrimental as pleasure.</note>;</l>
                    <l>For many things must make it be,</l>
                    <l>But one may make it less.</l>
                    <l>Nay, were our state as we would choose it,</l>
                    <l>'Twould be <ref target="a007" corresp="n007">consumed by fear to lose it</ref><note xml:id="n007" target="a007">We covet pleasure and happiness and thus are afraid of any other feeling or emotion.</note>.</l>
                </stanza>
                
                <stanza>
                    <l>What art thou, then, thou wingÃ«d air,</l>
                    <l><ref target="a008" corresp="n008">More weak and swift than fame?</ref><note xml:id="n008" target="a008">Pleasure is as fleeting as fame.</note></l>
                    <l>Whose next <ref target="a009" corresp="n009">successor is despair</ref><note xml:id="n009" target="a009">If one has a taste of pleasure, their life will fall into despair.</note>,</l>
                    <l>And its attendant shame.</l>
                    <l>Th' experienced prince then reason had</l>
                    <l>Who said of Pleasure,-<ref target="a010" corresp="n010">"It is mad."</ref><note xml:id="n010" target="a010">The constant drive for fleeting pleasure will only bring madness to those who seek it out.
